Project Overview

A Decade of Forest Change in the Western North

This study tracks land cover transformation in Asunafo North Municipality, Ghana, over a ten-year period from 2013 to 2023. The municipality sits within one of Ghana's most ecologically sensitive forest zones, making it a critical site for monitoring the pressures of agricultural expansion, urbanisation, and deforestation.

Using multi-temporal Landsat 8 imagery processed in Google Earth Engine, the analysis maps five land cover classes across three epochs — 2013, 2017, and 2023 — providing a detailed picture of how the landscape has changed and at what rate. Results are presented through classified maps, area statistics, and change detection charts designed to support conservation planning and policy decisions.

Objectives

What This Study Set Out to Do

  • 01 Classify land cover from Landsat 8 satellite imagery for 2013, 2017, and 2023 using supervised Random Forest classification in Google Earth Engine.
  • 02 Calculate the area and percentage coverage of each land cover class across all three epochs.
  • 03 Quantify net changes in each land cover class over the ten-year study period.
  • 04 Visualise results through classified maps, area statistics tables, and bar charts to communicate findings clearly.
Methodology

From Satellite to Map — The Workflow

The analysis followed a structured remote sensing workflow using cloud-based processing in Google Earth Engine and desktop GIS in QGIS.

01

Data Acquisition

Landsat 8 Level-2 surface reflectance imagery was sourced from USGS for three target years: 2013, 2017, and 2023. Only scenes with less than 10% cloud cover were selected to ensure data quality.

02

Image Processing in GEE

Imagery was loaded and processed in Google Earth Engine using JavaScript. Cloud masking was applied using the QA_PIXEL band, and composites were generated for each epoch before classification.

03

Supervised Classification

A Random Forest classifier was trained using manually digitised training samples for five land cover classes: Closed Forest, Open Forest, Urban, Water, and Bare Land. The trained model was applied to each epoch independently.

04

Export & Mapping in QGIS

Classified rasters were exported from GEE and imported into QGIS for cartographic mapping, area calculation, and production of the final classified land cover maps.

05

Statistical Analysis

Area statistics for each land cover class were computed and tabulated in Microsoft Excel. Change detection statistics and bar charts were produced to visualise class-level gains and losses across the study period.

Interpretation

Key Insights

🌲

Closed Forest Decline

Significant loss of closed forest cover was recorded over the study period, raising serious concerns for biodiversity, carbon sequestration, and watershed protection in this ecologically sensitive municipality.

🏘️

Rapid Urbanisation

Urban land cover expanded notably across all three epochs, reflecting population growth and the physical spread of settlements into previously vegetated areas at the municipality's fringes.

🌾

Agricultural Expansion

Open forest — which encompasses farmlands, grasslands, and shrubs — increased over the study period, consistent with smallholder agricultural expansion driving the conversion of natural vegetation.

📋

Policy Implications

The combined pressure of urban growth and agricultural expansion on forest resources underscores the urgent need for stronger land-use policies, forest reserve enforcement, and community-based conservation programmes in Asunafo North.
